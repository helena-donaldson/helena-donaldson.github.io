LOVE

[ləv]

1. affection based on common interests

2. an object of attachment, devotion, or admiration

3. a beloved person




we who span continents, aisles, ages, genders, races

do LOVE you, ONLINE.


we LOVE you, who are the

free thinkers

dreamers

believers

who build open source democracy

from a bedrock of binary

who through miraculous construct of 1s and 0s

lose physical form and become ONLINE.


but how might one become ONLINE?

as it is a state, not tool.

a framework, not thing.


simply put,

we must circle back to LOVE.


as one might LOVE thy neighbor,

one must LOVE LOVE itself;

for to become ONLINE is to embrace LOVE

in its strangest forms.


One must LOVE

their senile grandmother

who has reposted the same minion-themed meme

five times within the last month.


One must LOVE

the incessant chat rooms

whose Severance fan theories are so incoherently strange

that one questions if they even watch the same show.


One must LOVE

the true-crime podcasters

whose excitement at describing a decapitation

is almsot child-like in its glee.


strange, LOVE is.


but to be without a LOVE for another's LOVE

strange how it might be

is to be online but to not be ONLINE.


for to be ONLINE is to LOVE a community of LOVE,

mess and all,

and with a little

faith

trust

and JavaScript mixed in

surely we can all make something ONLINE

worth LOVING and fighting for.
